Introduction
North America Food Distribute (NA Food) is a family-owned food and restaurant supply importer and wholesaler located in West Sacramento, CA. They have minimum technology involvement. As a result, the management team can only conduct very basic analysis. They recently wanted to refine their database to manage their data to a single SKU level, so they can streamline their daily operation and decision-making process. Their daily activities are mainly conducted between clients,  the sales department, and warehouse. 


Scope
Although NA Food consists of three  main domains: Sales, Warehouse, and Purchasing, this project will be focusing on representing the sales and warehouse domain of NA Food. The scope of this project covers the most crucial detail of the company’s daily sales activity because direct sales make up 90% of the company’s total revenue. The sales department is the most important department of the company in which general and senior managers are promoted from. 
Business Rules
NA food offers a variety of products with 16 different categories in total, including sake, beer, hard liquor, soft drinks, frozen food, bulk dry food, seaweed, snacks, dry grocery, refrigerated grocery, soy sauce, seasoning, appliances, tableware, to-go containers, and sushi knives. The company requested that the database need to reflect different categories in their product ID. Therefore, one category will have at least one SKU, and one SKU belongs to one category. For example, ID starts with 14 means sake. Each SKU has one to many units. For example, Takara Junmai Sake has 6 different sizes, including 180ml, 375ml, 500ml, 720ml, 1.8l, and 18l. Since NA Food is a wholesaler, they stock a kind of product using several different suppliers. For example, they have two identical Tuna SKUs supplied by Azuma food and Ming Hong in case one is out of stock. This means there will be SKUs that have the same name but supplied or manufactured by different suppliers or manufacturers. An SKU belongs to one supplier or manufacturer, and one supplier or manufacturer has one to many SKUs. For orders, a client can place one to many orders through a sales representative. Each order has one to many order lines. Each sales representative has one to many clients. A client can only have one sales representative. 
Definitions
Products
Product table records products that are sold to clients. Attributes of products are the following: ProductID, ProductName, ManufacturerID,  CategoryID, and SupplierID.
Category
There are 16 categories: sake, beer, hard liquor, soft drinks, frozen food, bulk dry food, seaweed, snacks, dry grocery, refrigerated grocery, soy sauce, seasoning, appliances, tableware, to-go containers, and sushi knives. Attributes are CategoryID and CategoryName.
Unit
Each unit entry has ProductID, Unit, Cost, ListPrice, and AvailableQuantity. 
Manufacturer 
Manufacturers are businesses that produce all the inventory items. Attributes of manufacturers are the following: ManufacturerID, ManufacturerName, ManufacturerAddress(which is composed of Street, City, State, Country, and Zip Code), ManufacturerPhone, ManufacturerEmail, ManufacturerType (international or domestic)
Supplier
Suppliers are businesses that supply all the SKUs. Attributes of manufacturers are the following: SupplierID, SupplierName, SupplierAddress(which is composed of Street, City, State, Country, and Zip Code), SupplierPhone, SupplierEmail, SupplierType (international or domestic)
Client 
Clients are businesses who buy products from NA Food. Attributes are ClientID, ClientName, ClientAddress(which is composed of Street, City, State, Country, and Zip Code), ClientContact, ClientPhone, ManufacturerEmail, ClientTypes. There are two types of clients: grocery stores and restaurants.
SalesPerson
A salesperson represents the company, promotes SKUs to clients, and places orders for clients. Attributes include SalesPersonID and SalesPersonName.  
SalesOrder
Sales orders are placed through email, phone calls, or field visits by clients or sales representatives. Attributes of products are the following: OrderID, OrderDate, OrderAmount, DeliveryMethod (pickup or delivery), DeliveryDate, and SalesPerson (sales rep 1,2,3…etc). It uses ClientID as foreign key. SalesPerson needs its own table. The primary key will be Order ID, ClientID, SalesPersonID and Order Date.
OrderLine
OrderLIne, which records each product in an order. An order can have one to many order lines. Besides all info regarding a product, each order line also needs to include DiscountRate, SoldPrice, PurchaseQuantity and Amount.

Create Database
CREATE DATABASE IF NOT EXISTS NAFood;
USE NAFood;
CREATE TABLE Manufacturer (
    ManufacturerID INT PRIMARY KEY,
    ManufacturerName VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    Street VARCHAR(255),
    City VARCHAR(100),
    State VARCHAR(100),
    Country VARCHAR(100),
    ZipCode VARCHAR(20),
    ManufacturerPhone VARCHAR(20),
    ManufacturerEmail VARCHAR(255),
    ManufacturerType VARCHAR(20) CHECK (ManufacturerType IN ('international', 'domestic'))
);


CREATE TABLE Supplier (
    SupplierID INT PRIMARY KEY,
    SupplierName VARCHAR(255),
    Street VARCHAR(255),
    City VARCHAR(100),
    State VARCHAR(100),
    Country VARCHAR(100),
    ZipCode VARCHAR(20),  -- Similar to ManufacturerAddress
    SupplierPhone VARCHAR(50),
    SupplierEmail VARCHAR(100),
    SupplierType VARCHAR(50) CHECK (SupplierType IN ('domestic', 'international'))
);


CREATE TABLE Category (
    CategoryID INT PRIMARY KEY,
    CategoryName VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL
);




CREATE TABLE Product (
    ProductID INT PRIMARY KEY CHECK (ProductID BETWEEN 1000 AND 9999),
    ProductName VARCHAR(255),
    ManufacturerID INT,
    CategoryID INT,
        SupplierID INT,
    FOREIGN KEY (ManufacturerID) REFERENCES Manufacturer(ManufacturerID),
        FOREIGN KEY (CategoryID) REFERENCES Category(CategoryID),
    FOREIGN KEY (SupplierID) REFERENCES Supplier(SupplierID)
);






CREATE TABLE ProductUnit (
    ProductID INT,
    Unit VARCHAR(50),
    Cost DECIMAL(10, 2),
    ListPrice DECIMAL(10, 2),
    AvailableQuantity INT,
    PRIMARY KEY (ProductID, Unit),
    FOREIGN KEY (ProductID) REFERENCES Product(ProductID)
);


CREATE TABLE SalesPerson (
    SalesPersonID INT PRIMARY KEY,
    SalesPersonName VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL
);


CREATE TABLE Client (
    ClientID INT PRIMARY KEY,
    ClientName VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    Street VARCHAR(255),
    City VARCHAR(100),
    State VARCHAR(100),
    Country VARCHAR(100),
    ZipCode VARCHAR(20),
        SalesPersonID INT, 
    ClientContact VARCHAR(255),
    ClientPhone VARCHAR(20),
    ClientEmail VARCHAR(255),
    ClientType VARCHAR(20) CHECK (ClientType IN ('grocery store', 'restaurant')),
    FOREIGN KEY (SalesPersonID) REFERENCES SalesPerson(SalesPersonID)
);




CREATE TABLE SalesOrder (
    OrderID INT PRIMARY KEY,
    ClientID INT,
    SalesPersonID INT,
    OrderDate DATE,
    OrderTotal DECIMAL(10, 2),
    DeliveryMethod VARCHAR(20) CHECK (DeliveryMethod IN ('pickup', 'delivery')),
    DeliveryDate DATE,
    FOREIGN KEY (ClientID) REFERENCES Client (ClientID),
    FOREIGN KEY (SalesPersonID) REFERENCES SalesPerson(SalesPersonID)
);


CREATE TABLE OrderLine (
    OrderID INT,
    ProductID INT,
    Unit VARCHAR(50),
    DiscountRate DECIMAL(5, 2),  -- Discount as percentage (e.g., 10.00 for 10%)
    SoldPrice DECIMAL(10, 2),    -- Price of the product at the time of sale
    PurchaseQuantity INT,        -- Quantity of the product in the order line
    Amount DECIMAL(10, 2),       -- Total amount for the product line (SoldPrice * PurchaseQuantity)
    PRIMARY KEY (OrderID, ProductID, Unit),
    FOREIGN KEY (OrderID) REFERENCES SalesOrder(OrderID),
    FOREIGN KEY (ProductID, Unit) REFERENCES ProductUnit(ProductID, Unit)
);
show tables;

Demo Queries
1. The small packed Smoked Salmon is out of stock. The purchasing team wants to know who else supplies it
SELECT ProductName, Unit, Cost, ListPrice, AvailableQuantity, SupplierName
FROM Product p
JOIN ProductUnit pu
    ON p.ProductID = pu.ProductID
JOIN Supplier su
    ON p.SupplierID = su.SupplierID
WHERE SupplierName != 'MingHong'
  AND ProductName = 'Smoked Salmon'
  AND (Unit = '1kg Pack' OR Unit = '1lb Pack');
  

2. Finding the best selling SKU and its maker
SELECT m.manufacturername, p.productid, p.productname, SUM(ol.purchasequantity) TotalQuantity
FROM manufacturer m 
JOIN product p JOIN orderline ol
ON m.manufacturerId = p.manufacturerId
AND p.productid = ol.productid
WHERE p.productid = 
(SELECT p.productid
FROM product p JOIN orderline ol
ON p.productid = ol.productid
GROUP BY p.productid
ORDER BY SUM(ol.purchasequantity) DESC
LIMIT 1) 
GROUP BY m.manufacturername, p.productid, p.productname;
  

3. The company is planning to do a client appreciation dinner. Find out all clients who have spent over $1000. Order by descending order. 
SELECT 
    Client.ClientName, 
    (SELECT SUM(SalesOrder.OrderTotal) 
     FROM SalesOrder 
     WHERE SalesOrder.ClientID = Client.ClientID) AS SumOrderTotal
FROM 
    Client
WHERE 
    (SELECT SUM(SalesOrder.OrderTotal) 
     FROM SalesOrder 
     WHERE SalesOrder.ClientID = Client.ClientID) > 1000
ORDER BY 
    SumOrderTotal DESC;
  

4. The sales director would like to know who the top 3 performers are in the department.
SELECT sa.SalespersonName, cl.ClientName, t1.TotalOrderValue
FROM Salesperson sa
JOIN Client cl
        ON sa.SalespersonID = cl.SalespersonID
JOIN (
        SELECT so.SalespersonID, SUM(so.OrderTotal) AS TotalOrderValue
    FROM Salesorder so
    GROUP BY so.SalespersonID
    ORDER BY TotalOrderValue DESC
) t1 ON sa.SalespersonID = t1.SalespersonID
ORDER BY t1.TotalOrderValue DESC
LIMIT 3;
  

5. Find Products with the Highest Discount
SELECT 
    p.ProductName,
    pu.Unit,
    MAX(ol.DiscountRate) AS MaxDiscountRate
FROM 
    Product p
JOIN OrderLine ol
    ON p.ProductID = ol.ProductID
JOIN ProductUnit pu
    ON p.ProductID = pu.ProductID AND ol.Unit = pu.Unit
GROUP BY 
    p.ProductID, p.ProductName, pu.Unit
ORDER BY 
    MaxDiscountRate DESC
LIMIT 3;
